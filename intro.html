Everyone Starts with These Same Struggles

It's not just you; some people have had the right support structure and social framework to get started in the gym with ease. Others just haven't found the right tools or environment yet. It often comes down to circumstance as to whether or not someone gets in great shape-- and I want to be that system for you now.

Starting something new is always uncomfortable. In fitness, it feels like everyone knows what they're doing-- don't get lost in the noise and let's start small.

  • Have you walked into the gym and felt out of place? Maybe unsure of where to begin or what equipment to use?
  • Did you try random workout videos on YouTube? Get lost in the sea of advice on the internet?

Six Weeks to Sustainable Fitness

Don't follow a lifting routine without listening to your body-- instead, understand what goes into muscle growth, build confidence in yourself, and forge exercise identity to last a lifetime.

Start with actionable yet minimal changes to your routine. You'll barely notice the time investment, and it's easier to get started than you think--these changes will mean real results.

Weeks 1-2: Build Fitness Identity, Community, and Understand Growth

  • Start with actionable steps to shape your identity towards someone who prioritizes fitness.
  • Learn some basic bodyweight and kettlebell exercises to build familiarity with fundamental movement patterns.
  • Follow a simple planner to start stacking the habit of exercise and affirming your positive mindset.
  • Build community and get advice from me directly, plus other members!
  • Understand what actually goes into getting fit, versus spinning wheels in the gym.

Weeks 3-4: Find Motivation, Enjoyment, and Principles for Health

  • Tap into insights for building consistency and leveraging motivation and discipline.
  • Learn presence and mind-muscle connection for maximum enjoyment while lifting-- the chore becomes the reward.
  • Understand muscle balance, progressive overload, and how to sculpt your program so that you stay in great shape without promoting imbalance and injury.

Weeks 5-6: Solidify Confidence, Autonomy, and Extend Your Routine

  • Incorporate strategies to make exercise easier (and more enjoyable).
  • Learn to bridge the gaps that cause roadblocks in your fitness journey.
  • Break down reasons why you might be unfamiliar and anxious around the gym.
  • Elevate your routine and build your toolset around weight lifting.
  • Extend your practice confidently into the gym!
By the end of 6 weeks, you'll have built muscle and you'll feel confident with lifting. You'll be free of anxiety and confusion around what actually drives progress. Enjoy your newfound confidence and momentum!
